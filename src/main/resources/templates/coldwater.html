Coldwater fish are fish species that prefer to live in cooler water temperatures than most tropical fish. As far as aquarium fishes are concerned, cold-water species prefer water that is typically around or below 68° Fahrenheit. The most commonly kept cold-water pet fish are members of the Cyprinid family that includes Koi Carp. These kinds of fishes can be kept successfully in outdoor ponds, surviving the cold of winter by hiding away in vegetation and the warmer depths of their habitat when the surface of the water freezes.
